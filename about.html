Who is Bryan Totty?

I have had the opportunity to lead international business teams, hike remote mountainsides, perform in front of large audiences, and study wealth generation and investing. My friends describe me as driven and a supportive mentor. I aim to inspire others to explore beyond their perceived limitations.

Career

I currently lead large-scale GRC cloud security programs across Azure Storage at Microsoft — directing compliance and risk alignment to NIST 800-53, SOC 2, FedRAMP, and GDPR across 40+ engineering teams. I build AI-powered workflows to automate reporting, analyze security trends, and assist with secure code reviews, aligning with DevSecOps principles.

Prior to Microsoft, I served as a Security Enterprise Technical Program Manager at Meta, where I led programs at massive scale — migrating 30K+ VDI clients for global operations, driving a legal hold program across 100K+ devices, and championing a secure USB rollout impacting 10K+ users.

I spent over 10 years at Red Hat, first as a Security Senior Technical Support Engineer leading 7 teams, then as Senior Security Technical Program Manager overseeing security processes across 16 products with 200+ stakeholders. I led the rollout of a new PSIRT Incident Response framework, drove the Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) program, onboarded 3 acquired companies into Red Hat's security processes, and evangelized security at events across the US.

I started my career at IBM as a Server Test Engineer, testing prototype server hardware and software in a multi-million-dollar R&D laboratory. LinkedIn.

Music

By age 25, I owned a record label — performing live shows, doing radio station interviews, building marketing strategies, and professionally recording myself and clients.

When I started in music, there were no music streaming platforms, YouTube did not exist, and social media was not a thing. At that time, you had to hustle and network in person at shows and meet owners of venues through weeks, months, and years of dedication. We were on the streets of college campuses handing out fliers and self promoting our bands. I learned how to network and be persistent because the exposure route required getting on a radio station or TV in order to reach fans.

I have always enjoyed creating in a way that allows me to express myself and impact others. With my solo rap music, I really dug deep into the lyrical techniques and always challenged myself to rap in different styles and try an array of different music from classic hip-hop to trap to rock music. Through my music, I embrace a range of ways to connect people from fun all the way to mysterious and dark music.

As a side project, I recorded and performed two music videos for the enterprise open source software company Red Hat.
